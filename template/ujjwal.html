Your Donation Can Give Someone a Dignified Livelihood

A country like India which has a huge young population can reap better benefits from the demographic dividend if its youth are better skilled and employable. According to some estimates, India’s demographic dividend window opened in 2005-06 and it is expected to last till the mid-2050s. By 2047, India will have 1.1 billion people in the working age group (15-64), which is the peak of the demographic dividend that the country is expected to reach in the coming years. And, this is why the next two decades are extremely crucial for India.

Smile Foundation, recognizing the need of the hour, has reprogrammed its employability training initiative 'STeP' to fight the challenges posed by poverty and unemployment. Our campaign ‘Tayyari Kal Ki’ aims at Training & Up-skilling the youth between the age of 18-32 years for employment and empowering them with a secure livelihood.

This livelihood mission is in strong alignment with the Government of India’s ‘Skill India’ initiative. Currently, the programme is operational through 74 centres across 7 states. In the last 15 years, Smile Foundation has trained over 90,000 youth, and placed more than 56,500 of them in over 400 brands across India.
